Feeling unsure (the big questions)

Do I need to know anything about the Bible before I come?

No. You don't need any prior knowledge at all. Bible Conversations are designed for people who are completely new, as well as those who are just curious or revisiting questions they've had before.

Do I have to speak or join in?

No. You're free to take part as much or as little as you like. Many people come along just to listen at first. There's no expectation to speak, read aloud, or answer questions.

What if I don't believe in the Bible or Christianity?

That's absolutely fine. Most people who come are exploring or unsure. Bible Conversations are a space to ask honest questions and hear different perspectives, not a place where you're expected to already believe something.

Is there any pressure to come back or commit to anything?

No. There's no pressure to return or commit to anything beyond the conversation you attend. You're free to come once and decide for yourself what you think.

Can I just come once to try it?

Yes. Many people come along just to see what it's like. You can treat it as a one-off visit.

What actually happens

What happens at a Bible Conversation?

At a Bible Conversation, a small group of people meet to read a short part of the Bible together and talk about it. The aim is simply to understand what the passage says and explore the questions it raises about life, people, and meaning.

How long does a Bible Conversation last?

Most conversations last around 60-90 minutes. Each event will list a clear start and finish time so you know what to expect before you arrive.

What is the group like?

Groups are usually informal and friendly. They are built around conversation, listening, and questions rather than lectures.

Who leads the conversation?

A local host or facilitator guides the conversation, keeps things moving, and makes sure people have space to contribute without feeling put on the spot.

Practical details

Do I need to book in advance?

Where booking is available, use the Eventbrite link on the event card. Booking helps the host know numbers, but each event page will make clear what is needed.

Can I bring a friend who hasn't booked?

Usually yes, but it is best to check the event details first. If there is a booking link, your friend can book there too.

Are Bible Conversations free?

Yes. Bible Conversations are free to attend unless an individual event clearly says otherwise.

Common concerns

Will I be asked to pray or read out loud?

No. You can simply listen. You will not be forced to pray, read aloud, or answer a question.

Is this part of a church service?

No. A Bible Conversation is a smaller, discussion-based gathering. Some happen in churches, but the format is not a church service.

About the organisers

Who runs Bible Conversations Teesside?

Bible Conversations Teesside is run with local churches and hosts who want to create welcoming spaces for people to explore the Bible.

Who is Bible Society?

Bible Society is a charity that helps people engage with the Bible in ways that are accessible, thoughtful, and relevant to everyday life.
