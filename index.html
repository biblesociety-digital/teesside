What is Bible Conversations: Teesside?

It’s a space to meet with people just like you: those who are interested in finding out a little bit more about the Bible.

Free, non-preachy and non-pressured, Bible Conversations: Teesside is the place to figure out how you feel about the Bible – somewhere to explore this ancient text for yourself and hear what others think about it.

Who are we?

Bible Conversations: Teesside is an community-focused initiative that’s all about making the Bible accessible and relatable for anyone who wants to find out more about it – but doesn’t want to told what to think about it.

It’s a joint initiative between UK-based charity, Bible Society and churches in Teesside.

Do I need to book in advance?

You’re absolutely welcome to rock up and join a session near you. But we recommend booking via Eventbrite, so your hosts are prepped for the right number.

Is it free to join?

Yes, Bible Conversations are completely free. Simple as that.

How do I sign up?

Registration is quick and easy.

Locate a Bible Conversation group that works for you via our map.

It'll take you through to the Eventbrite page, where you can register.

Keep an eye on your inbox for any information you need once you've signed up.

Can I bring a friend along?

Definitely - the more the merrier.

Why not share this link and invite them to sign up, so they know what to expect?

What happens during the sessions?

Settle in. Read the Bible. Discuss.

In the first session, your hosts will give you a heads up on how Bible Conversations works. There might be a short icebreaker, or you’ll be invited to introduce yourself so you can get to know the group.

After a short, informal chat, you’ll read or listen to an audio version of a Bible story.

Your host will guide you through a few questions as a group, including the likes of:

  • What do you think the story is about?
  • What do you like or dislike about the story?
  • What does this story teach us about God?
  • What does it reveal about people or about you?
  • How does this story challenge or encourage you?

How long do Bible Conversations last?

Around 45 minutes. They’ll be wrapped up by the hour.
